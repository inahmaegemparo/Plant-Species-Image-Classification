# Plant-Species-Image-Classifications

# --------------A. Project Overview--------------

● Brief description of the project

This project uses Teachable Machine to build an image classification model for identifying different plant species. By training on labeled datasets, the system learns to recognize unique botanical features and patterns. Once trained, the model can accurately classify new images and provide a real-time confidence score for its predictions.

● Purpose of the image classification model

The purpose of this model is to automate botanical identification through image analysis. By recognizing unique patterns and features, it provides an accurate digital tool to distinguish plant species and prevent misidentification.

20 MEDICINAL PLANTS

## 1. Katakataka

  Common Name: Katakataka

  Scientific Name: Bryophyllum pinnatum

It is often called the "Life Plant" because new   plantlets can sprout from the edges of a single leaf even after it is detached. It is traditionally used as a poultice for wounds, burns, and to relieve headaches.

## 2. Turmeric

  <img width="275" height="183" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a0ef7794-c74b-4395-b500-60d0c7e632e5" />

Common Name: Turmeric (Luyang Dilaw)

Scientific Name: Curcuma longa

Description: A leafy perennial in the ginger family featuring large, green blades and a signature bright orange-yellow rhizome. It is globally famous as a spice and a powerful medicinal herb, highly valued for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory "curcumin" content.


## 3. Makahiya

<img width="259" height="194" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4c894894-924b-4e4b-ac38-eafaf4a79ed1" />

Common Name: Sensitive Plant / Touch-me-not

Scientific Name: Mimosa pudica

Description: A low-growing, creeping perennial legume famous for its "shyness." Its compound leaves fold inward and droop when touched or shaken, defending itself from herbivores. It features prickly stems and small, fluffy, pink spherical flower heads.

## 4. Candle Bush

<img width="259" height="194" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ebec746a-3028-464d-b33d-10a93b6a2cd7" />

Common Name: Candle Bush (Akapulko)

Scientific Name: Senna alata

Description: An ornamental and medicinal shrub named for its upright yellow flower spikes that resemble waxy candles. It has large, oblong leaflets and is widely used in tropical medicine as a potent antifungal treatment for skin conditions like ringworm.


## 5. Madagascar Periwinkle

<img width="259" height="194"  alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1705dcc7-7f4e-4467-aaf5-7848355751ae" />

Common Name: Madagascar Periwinkle (Chichirica)

Scientific Name: Catharanthus roseus

Description: An evergreen subshrub with glossy, dark green leaves and five-petaled flowers that come in shades of pink, white, or rosy-purple. While popular in gardens, it is scientifically significant as the source of alkaloids used in chemotherapy.


## 6. Yerba Buena

<img width="259" height="194"  alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b54f7770-531a-4734-810d-705545affa7f" />

Common Name: Yerba Buena

Scientific Name: Clinopodium douglasii

Description: A sprawling, aromatic herb belonging to the mint family. It is characterized by its small, scalloped leaves and refreshing scent. It is commonly used as a herbal remedy for aches, pains, and digestive issues.

## 7. Sambong

<img width="259" height="194"  alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f4b07e9c-cfc8-48e1-ba77-98140061fbb7" />

Common Name: Sambong

Scientific Name: Blumea balsamifera

Description: A tall, hairy, half-woody herb that emits a strong camphor-like odor. The leaves are soft and serrated. In traditional medicine, it is highly valued as a diuretic and for its ability to help dissolve kidney stones.

## 8. Abgaw



Common Name: Abgaw (Alagaw)

Scientific Name: Premna odorata

Description: A small tree or shrub with aromatic, broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is traditionally used in the Philippines to treat coughs and respiratory ailments.

## 9. Hilbas



Common Name: Hilbas (Damong Maria)

Scientific Name: Artemisia vulgaris

Description: Also known as Mugwort, this is an erect perennial herb with deeply lobed, aromatic leaves that are green on top and silvery-white underneath. It is frequently used in folk medicine to regulate menstruation and ease digestive discomfort.

## 10. Lagundi


Common Name: Lagundi

Scientific Name: Vitex negundo

Description: A large shrub with distinct finger-like (palmate) leaves, usually composed of five leaflets. It produces small blue to lavender flowers. It is one of the most clinically validated medicinal plants, widely used as a cough remedy and anti-asthma medication.

## 11. Sambang Getih


Common Name: Sambang Getih

Scientific Name: Hemigraphis alternata

Description: A low-creeping herb often used as groundcover. It is striking for its metallic, silvery-purple leaves that are deep red underneath. It is traditionally used to promote wound healing and check bleeding.

## 12. Papaya Leaves
Common Name: Papaya Leaves

Scientific Name: Carica papaya

Description: Large, deeply palmately lobed leaves belonging to the papaya tree. The leaves contain a milky latex and are rich in enzymes like papain. They are often used in traditional medicine to increase platelet counts and as a digestive aid.

## 13. Sampasampalukan


Common Name: Sampasampalukan

Scientific Name: Phyllanthus niruri

Description: A small, erect herb that looks like a miniature tamarind tree due to its small, thin leaflets arranged in rows. It is famously known as "Stonebreaker" because of its traditional use in treating kidney and gallbladder stones.

## 14. Yahong-yahong


Common Name: Yahong-yahong (Gotu Kola)

Scientific Name: Centella asiatica

Description: A small, creeping herb with kidney-shaped or fan-shaped leaves. It is renowned in herbalism as a "brain food" for cognitive health and for its skin-healing properties.

## 15. Saluyot


Common Name: Saluyot

Scientific Name: Corchorus olitorius

Description: A tall, leafy vegetable characterized by its slightly serrated leaves with distinct "tails" at the base. When cooked, the leaves have a mucilaginous (slimy) texture. It is highly nutritious and rich in antioxidants.

## 16. Ginger


Common Name: Ginger (Luya)

Scientific Name: Zingiber officinale

Description: A herbaceous perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and yellowish flowers. It is primarily grown for its pungent, aromatic rhizome (underground stem), which is a staple in both culinary dishes and as a remedy for nausea and inflammation.

## 17. Hauili


Common Name: Hauili

Scientific Name: Ficus septica

Description: A small tree or shrub belonging to the fig family, characterized by large, glossy, opposite leaves with prominent veins. It produces small, stalkless figs along its branches and is often used in traditional medicine to treat skin diseases and rheumatism.

## 18. Mansanitas


Common Name: Mansanitas (Aratiles)

Scientific Name: Muntingia calabura

Description: Also known as the Jamaican Cherry, this is a fast-growing tree with drooping branches and soft, hairy leaves. It produces small white flowers and sweet, red, berry-like fruits that resemble tiny apples.


## 19. Pomelo Leaves


Common Name: Pomelo Leaves

Scientific Name: Citrus maxima

Description: Large, shiny, dark green leaves with winged petioles. The leaves carry the characteristic citrus scent of the pomelo fruit and are often boiled in water for aromatic baths or used in traditional poultices to reduce swelling.


## 20. Bittervine


Common Name: Bittervine (locally known as Hagonoy in some regions)

Scientific Name: Mikania micrantha

Description: A fast-growing, perennial creeping vine that belongs to the sunflower family. It is easily identified by its heart-shaped leaves and dense clusters of small, white to greenish flowers. While often considered an invasive "mile-a-minute" weed, it is used in traditional medicine for its ability to stop bleeding from minor cuts (hemostatic property) and for its antimicrobial and analgesic effects on skin itches and wounds.
